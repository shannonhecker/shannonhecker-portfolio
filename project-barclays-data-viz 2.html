Barclays Data Visualisation

End-to-end product design for an equities dashboard: global filters, multi-series charts, a price-move heatmap, and responsive layouts that stay true to Barclays patterns while keeping dense market data scannable.

about.

Project Summary

Context. Equity and sector specialists need to compare positions, spot drift, and validate stories without hunting across screens. The product opportunity was to bring EoD prices, daily change, sector trends, and cross-name moves into one coherent surface inside the Barclays shell.

Approach. I framed the job as scan-first analysis: establish hierarchy, then layer charts and tables so power users get density while typography and colour semantics keep gains, losses, and neutral states instantly readable.

My Role

I owned information architecture, the dashboard grid, chart and table treatments, and breakpoint behaviour from desktop through narrow viewports. Deliverables included annotated layouts and component-level specs so engineering could align implementation with the intended scanning path and accessibility of the data.

Design Process

Solution. The desktop experience anchors on global filters (company, sector, date range) and a modular card system: summary tiles, multi-line and bar charts, sector area views, a price-change heatmap, and per-stock sparklines so users can triage before drilling in.

Responsive behaviour. The narrow layout reflows the same modules into a single column with preserved reading order and chart legibility, so analysts get a consistent mental model whether they are at a desk or on a constrained display.

Outcomes
